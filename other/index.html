  One music writing experience of mine has a major meaning in my life as a whole. It began in the wake of the 2019 STEM School Highlands Ranch Shooting, where many friends, acquaintances, and peers were dealing with the post traumatic maelstrom surrounding our school. I had known Kendrick Castillo, and the events of that day, the sounds of the gunshots, and the spots of blood on the floor. I was there when it happened and I had friends that were in the room that it happened. In the wake of this event, many from my school were struck with not only severe anxiety, but as we all contintued to go to school every day after it had happened, we went through the pain of coming back to the spot where it had all happened for the rest of the year. One thing I struggled with in this period was maintaining motivation and finishing out the school year high. After it had happened I was unable to put pen to paper, and felt numb day after day, in a state of unfeeling grief. It all didn't matter anymore, not school and not anything. It took nearly until the end of the year to reinvigorate myself and even then, school was chaos. Three days before the end of the school year, my music teacher the past year, Brent Roach, had reached out to me asking for my assistance. He wanted to create a song, not only in honor of Kendrick, but in honor of all the students, teachers, and people who had gone through this with us all. I accepted. The challenge of this was that we wanted to have every student in the schools voice to sing in the song. I had three days. To gather a crew to record everyone, chart out what teachers we would go to, to finish the song and get it to everyone involved. I worked from 7 am to 9 pm for the next 2 days, planning everything and orchestrating everything. Just before the end of school, everyone was recorded and put into the music, as a last unifying force. This song is attached below. If you listen through it, it begins with the music teacher, Brent Roach, singing "The most beautiful roads lead to the most beautiful places" - which was Kendricks senior quote - before expanding on the theme. In the middle of the song, everyone joins in, in chorus. "You've got to live". Other renditions have been preformed, but this was the original and it was how at the end of the year, the students at STEM showed their Unity. This project was not only an artistic challenge, but a logistical one as well and in the end, we came through. Heres to us!
